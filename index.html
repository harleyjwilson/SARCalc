Naismith's Rule - Determination of Possible Search Area

Trip Times

It is a general rule for calculating trip times for the average walker with a medium weight back travelling over flat terrain.

    Easy going over open country: 5 km/hour
    Easy scrambling over rocky ground: 3 km/hour
    Rough going (thick scrub, heavy sand): 1 to 1.5 km/hour

Difficulty Complications

When travelling for extended periods or traversing hilly country, the search radius needs to be reduced to allow for fatigue and slower travel.

    For every 5 hours of walking: subtract the distance travelled in 1 hour
    For every 500m of ascent or 1000m of descent over the estimated distance travelled: subtract the distance travelled in 1 hour
